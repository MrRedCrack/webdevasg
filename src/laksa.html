Laksa – Malaysia’s Spicy and Aromatic Noodle Soup

A flavorful fusion of coconut, spices, and noodles.

Introduction

Laksa is a beloved Malaysian street food — a spicy noodle soup combining creamy coconut milk, aromatic spices, and fresh herbs. Served with rice noodles or vermicelli, tender chicken or prawns, bean sprouts, and a squeeze of lime, Laksa offers a perfect balance of savory, tangy, and spicy flavors.

History & Cultural Significance

Laksa’s origins can be traced to the Peranakan culture, a blend of Chinese and Malay heritage. Over time, different regions in Malaysia have developed their own versions — from the rich, coconut-based curry laksa of the west coast to the tangy, tamarind-infused asam laksa of Penang. It remains a symbol of cultural fusion and comfort food for many Malaysians.

Key Ingredients

Ingredient Description
Rice Noodles / Vermicelli Main carbohydrate base of the soup
Coconut Milk Creates the creamy, rich broth
Laksa Paste Blend of spices, chili, lemongrass, and shrimp paste
Prawns / Chicken Common protein choices for laksa
Bean Sprouts & Fresh Herbs Add crunch and freshness to the dish
Lime & Sambal Provide acidity and extra heat

How to Make Laksa

  1. Boil noodles or vermicelli until cooked, then set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pot, add laksa paste, and fry until fragrant.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and chicken or seafood stock, bringing to a gentle simmer.
  4. Add prawns, chicken, or tofu, and cook until done.
  5. Assemble by placing noodles in a bowl, ladling hot broth over, and topping with bean sprouts, herbs, lime, and sambal.

Fun Facts

  • Penang Asam Laksa was ranked 7th in CNN Travel’s “World’s 50 Best Foods” in 2011.
  • There are over 10 regional variations of Laksa across Malaysia.
