Roti Canai Recipe

Ingredient:

  • All-purpose flour: 500g
  • Salt: 1 tsp
  • Sugar: 1 tbsp
  • Water: 250ml (approximately)
  • Evaporated milk: 50ml
  • Egg: 1
  • Butter or margarine: 50g (melted)
  • Vegetable oil: for coating and frying

Instructions:

Prepare the Dough:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix the water, evaporated milk, and egg until well combined.
  3. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing with your hands or a dough hook until it forms a dough.
  4. Add the melted butter to the dough and knead until it becomes smooth and elastic. This should take about 10-15 minutes.

Rest the Dough:

  1. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a golf ball (you should get around 10-12 balls).
  2. Coat each ball with vegetable oil and place them on a tray.
  3. Cover the tray with a damp cloth and let the dough rest for at least 4 hours, or overnight for better results.

Shape the Roti Canai:

  1. Lightly oil your hands and the work surface.
  2. Take one dough ball and flatten it slightly with your hands. Then, using both hands, stretch the dough outwards from the center to create a thin, almost transparent sheet. The thinner, the better.
  3. Fold the dough inwards from the edges to form a square or round shape.

Cook the Roti Canai:

  1. Heat a griddle or a non-stick pan over medium heat.
  2. Add a little vegetable oil or butter to the pan.
  3. Place the shaped dough onto the hot pan and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Repeat with the remaining dough balls.

Serve:

  1. Once cooked, you can serve the roti canai with a side of dhal, curry, or your favorite dipping sauce.
  2. For a fluffier texture, clap the roti canai between your hands after cooking to create layers.
  3. Enjoy your homemade Roti Canai!
